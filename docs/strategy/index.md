# Strategy

Secondary material for product framing, long-term thesis, and research direction.

Strategy answers "why this product?" and "what long-range bet are we making?" It is directional material, not the source of truth for shipped behavior, and it should not be read as a promise that every thesis idea is on the current product roadmap.

## How to read this section

| If you need...                        | Go here                                                    | Why                                                                    |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Shipped language behavior             | [Language Reference](../reference/index.md)                | The authoritative contract for shipped syntax, semantics, and limits   |
| Learning and onboarding               | [Guides](../guides/index.md)                               | Tutorials and conceptual walkthroughs for the shipped runtime          |
| Current evidence and caveats          | [Evaluation](../evaluation/index.md)                       | The public evidence set for what works today and where the gaps remain |
| Backlog status and near-term tracking | [Roadmap](../roadmap.md)                                   | `.beads`-backed shipped, tracked, and exploratory status               |
| Active proposals and planning packs   | [WIP Features](../wip/index.md)                            | Unshipped design work, imported packs, and design history              |
| Research evidence behind the strategy | [Research Reports](../research/README.md)                  | External-source syntheses that inform product direction                |
| Repo-wide doc boundary rules          | [Documentation Governance](../documentation-governance.md) | How to interpret strategy, roadmap, research, WIP, and reference pages |

Read this section after you understand the shipped product surface. The thesis pages are intentionally broader than the current product contract.
